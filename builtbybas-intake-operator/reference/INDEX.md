# Reference Library

The operator's knowledge library. The operator does NOT load all of this at once. It reads
identity.md and rules.md every time, then pulls only the entries a given inquiry needs: the
matching industry profile, the regulation that got flagged. This keeps context lean and
decisions fast. It is the ICM layered-scoping discipline applied to the operator's own brain.

## How the operator uses the library

1. identity.md and rules.md: always. The role and the procedure.
2. service-catalog.md: validate fit and reframe. What BuiltByBas does and does not do.
3. routing-map.md: commit to one of the ten outcomes.
4. decision-rubric.md: score complexity, fit, and risk.
5. industry-profiles.md: load the ONE profile matching the inquiry's industry, its business, data, compliance, and reframe tells.
6. regulations.md: look up any specific regulation that gets flagged, by name.

Load on demand. A consumer landscaping inquiry does not need the HIPAA entry; a dental inquiry
does. Pull the entry, use it, move on.

## Catalog

| Entry | What it holds | Load when |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| service-catalog.md | the 12 tracks plus decline and refer, with boundaries | every inquiry (fit and reframe) |
| routing-map.md | the 10 outcomes and their destinations | every inquiry (commit) |
| decision-rubric.md | complexity, fit, risk, and confidence scoring | every inquiry (score) |
| industry-profiles.md | per-vertical business, data, compliance, reframe tells, and fit | when an industry is named or implied (load the one) |
| regulations.md | each regulation: what it is, who it hits, what to flag | when a specific regulation is triggered |

## Growing the library

The operator reads this catalog, so a new entry is usable the moment it is listed here. Add
entries as patterns recur. Candidates not yet built: more verticals, a response-template
library, a decisions archive for calibration, a glossary of BuiltByBas terms.
