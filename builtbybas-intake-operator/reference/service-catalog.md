# Service Catalog

The definitive list of what BuiltByBas does. The operator validates every inquiry against
this catalog. It is the backbone of the reframe decision: to know that a client asked for the
wrong thing, the operator first has to know exactly what the right things are.

Two groups. Group 1 is the productized build work the public site sells. Group 2 is the
operational work that is usually the REAL need hiding behind a build request. Most reframes
move an inquiry from Group 1 language into a Group 2 track.

Complexity labels used throughout match the BuiltByBas internal scale: Simple, Moderate,
Complex, Enterprise.

---

## Group 1: Build services (productized, on the public site)

### Custom Website

- What it is: a custom, responsive, SEO-optimized website built to convert visitors into customers. (Public site currently labels this "Marketing Website"; Bas is renaming it to "Custom Website" to avoid colliding with marketing-the-service, which BuiltByBas refers out to Marketing partner. The operator should recognize both names.)
- Typical price band: $2,500 to $8,000.
- Default complexity: Simple to Moderate.
- Route here when: the client needs a website built, a public presence that builds credibility and captures leads.
- Not this when: the "site" is actually an internal tool (-> Business Dashboard), the real problem is a broken process (-> Workflow Design), or the client wants their marketing run for them, ads, campaigns, SEO management, social (-> Refer to Marketing partner, see Out of scope).

### Website Redesign

- What it is: a performance-optimized rebuild of an existing site that respects the brand while modernizing it.
- Typical price band: $3,000 to $10,000.
- Default complexity: Moderate.
- Route here when: a site already exists and the ask is to elevate it. Expect a current-site URL.
- Not this when: there is no existing site (-> Custom Website), or the redesign is a pretext for new functionality (check Business Dashboard / Full Platform).

### Landing Page

- What it is: a high-conversion single-page site built for one purpose, turning traffic into leads.
- Typical price band: $1,000 to $3,000.
- Default complexity: Simple.
- Route here when: one campaign, one offer, one CTA, with a known traffic source.
- Not this when: they need multiple pages or ongoing content (-> Custom Website); or the ask is a general business presence that merely happens to be small (-> Custom Website, Simple). "One-page" describes form, not purpose: a small business wanting a general presence is a Simple Custom Website even if it fits on one page. Route Landing Page only for a genuine single campaign/offer/CTA.

### Business Dashboard

- What it is: a custom admin panel or internal tool that gives real-time visibility into operations.
- Typical price band: $5,000 to $20,000.
- Default complexity: Complex.
- Route here when: the client describes something INTERNAL, "for my team," reporting, real-time data, or replacing a spreadsheet they run the business on.
- Not this when: the data is a mess before it can be shown (-> Data Organization first), or they want clients to log in (-> Client Portal).

### Client Portal

- What it is: a branded, client-facing window into projects, deliverables, messaging, invoices, bookings.
- Typical price band: $4,000 to $15,000.
- Default complexity: Complex.
- Route here when: the client's own customers need a logged-in space.
- Not this when: the login is for internal staff (-> Business Dashboard).

### E-Commerce

- What it is: a custom, ground-up storefront (not a Shopify template) built around products, brand, customers.
- Typical price band: $8,000 to $25,000.
- Default complexity: Complex.
- Route here when: selling physical, digital, subscription, or service products online is the core.
- Not this when: a template store genuinely fits and budget is tiny (boundary call, may be out of scope).

### CRM System

- What it is: a custom CRM to capture leads, track deals, and follow up on time, built to how the client works.
- Typical price band: $8,000 to $25,000.
- Default complexity: Complex.
- Route here when: the pain is lead/deal tracking and follow-up.
- Not this when: the sales PROCESS itself is undefined (-> Workflow Design first), or it is one piece of a bigger ops need (-> Full Operations Platform).

### Full Operations Platform

- What it is: website + CRM + client portal + invoicing + AI tools unified into one custom platform that runs the business.
- Typical price band: $15,000 to $50,000+.
- Default complexity: Enterprise.
- Route here when: the client describes running their whole business through one connected system, or names three or more of the build services at once.
- Not this when: it is really one component dressed up big, or the operation is not yet mapped (-> Workflow Design first).

### AI-Powered Tools

- What it is: custom AI assistants, content generators, and automation tools, built with responsible-AI review (human approves every output before it reaches a client).
- Typical price band: $10,000 to $40,000.
- Default complexity: Complex to Enterprise.
- Route here when: the leverage is genuine AI (generation, classification, assistance) with a human in the loop.
- Not this when: the win is a deterministic automation with no AI needed (-> Workflow Design), or the request fails the ethics screen (-> Decline).

## Group 2: Operational tracks (the usual reframe targets, not yet on the public site)

These are the real BuiltByBas value: removing the friction in how a business runs. Clients
rarely arrive asking for these by name. They ask for a "website" or a "system" and describe
one of these. The operator's job is to catch that and reframe.

### Data Organization

- What it is: taking scattered, siloed, or messy business data (spreadsheets, exports, inboxes, disconnected tools) and structuring it into something clean, queryable, and trustworthy.
- Engagement: scoped per project. (Confirm typical range with Bas.)
- Default complexity: Moderate to Complex.
- The real need behind: "my data is everywhere," "I can't find anything," "the numbers never match," "I have five spreadsheets that should be one."
- Often a PREREQUISITE: a Business Dashboard or CRM built on messy data fails, so Data Organization frequently routes first, then the build.

### Workflow Design

- What it is: mapping how work actually flows, finding where it stalls or goes manual, and designing the process (and any automation or tooling) that removes the friction.
- Engagement: scoped per project. (Confirm typical range with Bas.)
- Default complexity: Moderate to Complex.
- The real need behind: "we keep doing X by hand," "it takes forever," "things fall through the cracks," "I am the only one who can do this," "I want a website to fix [an internal process]."
- This is the single most common reframe target. "Build me a site/tool" is very often "fix my process."

### User Training

- What it is: getting the people who use a system (the client's staff, or the client's own users and customers) able to actually use it, through documentation, SOPs, and hands-on enablement.
- Engagement: scoped per project. (Confirm typical range with Bas.)
- Default complexity: Simple to Moderate.
- The real need behind: "no one knows how to use it," "it only works when I do it," "we bought the tool but nobody adopted it," "my team (or my users) need training on [system]."
- Often a FOLLOW-ON: pairs with a build or a workflow engagement to make it stick.

## Out of scope (Decline and Referral outcomes)

Three ways an inquiry lands outside BuiltByBas. All are real decisions, not punts.

1. Ethics screen (firm, non-negotiable, from the BuiltByBas AI policy). Decline any project
   involving: surveillance without consent, deceptive practices, discrimination, dark
   patterns, or exploitation. No reframe, no consultation. Decline with a clear reason.
2. Marketing services -> Refer to Marketing partner. Marketing is outside Bas's
   wheelhouse. When the real need is marketing the business rather than building software
   (ad campaigns, SEO-as-a-service, social media management, brand or content strategy),
   refer the client to our marketing partner. This is a warm referral, not a cold decline.
   Look-alike to watch: "I need marketing" often means "I need a website to market myself"
   (-> Custom Website, BuiltByBas), not marketing services. Read the inquiry to tell which.
3. Genuine non-fit. A real, legitimate need that BuiltByBas does not deliver and Marketing partner
   does not cover (for example a purely physical/offline job, or pure graphic-design-only with
   no build). Decline and, where possible, recommend a better-suited alternative.
